## Search 1 — 2026-03-25 (Phase 2: Research Design)
- **Search purpose**: Identify closest papers + build theoretical framework
- **Keywords**: "Emerging Market for Intelligence" NBER 34608; "Demand for LLMs" Fradkin; LLM API market competition pricing; creative destruction new product displacement; vertical differentiation quality ladder; open source proprietary competition; Oster 2019 coefficient stability
- **Found papers**:
  1. Demirer, Fradkin, Tadelis & Peng (2025) "The Emerging Market for Intelligence: Pricing, Supply, and Demand for LLMs" NBER WP 34608 — **PRIMARY REFERENCE**: Documents 6 facts about LLM market structure using OpenRouter + Azure data. Key facts: rapid growth driven by open-source, 90% price gap OS vs closed, price elasticity ~1, multi-model usage growing but most firms concentrate on one. Uses similar data source.
  2. Fradkin (2025) "Demand for LLMs: Descriptive Evidence on Substitution, Market Expansion, and Multihoming" arXiv:2504.15440 — **CLOSEST PAPER**: Uses OpenRouter data (Jan-Apr 2025) to study 3 model releases (Claude 3.7, Gemini 2.0 Flash, Gemini 2.5 Pro). Documents (1) rapid adoption stabilizing in weeks, (2) heterogeneity in substitution vs market expansion, (3) widespread multihoming. Our contribution: systematic panel analysis of ALL entries, not just 3 case studies.
  3. Bresnahan & Greenstein (1999) "Technological Competition and the Structure of the Computer Industry" J. Industrial Economics — Platform displacement in computing, switching costs. We study displacement at much higher frequency.
  4. Bresnahan, Greenstein & Henderson (2011) "Schumpeterian Competition and Diseconomies of Scope" NBER — IBM/Microsoft displacement dynamics.
  5. Baron (2020) "Vertical Differentiation, Product Innovation, and Dynamic Competition" J. Economics & Management Strategy — Theory of quality ladder competition with vertical differentiation.
  6. Oster (2019) "Unobservable Selection and Coefficient Stability" J. Business & Economic Statistics — Sensitivity analysis methodology for coefficient stability bounds.
  7. Shaked & Sutton (1982, 1983) — Foundational vertical differentiation theory.
  8. de Chaisemartin & D'Haultfœuille (2020) "Two-Way Fixed Effects Estimators with Heterogeneous Treatment Effects" AER — TWFE bias with staggered treatment.
  9. Sun & Abraham (2021) "Estimating Dynamic Treatment Effects in Event Studies with Heterogeneous Treatment Effects" J. Econometrics — Interaction-weighted estimator.
  10. Goodman-Bacon (2021) "Difference-in-Differences with Variation in Treatment Timing" Econometrica — Decomposition of TWFE.

- **Verification status**: Papers 1-2 verified via NBER and arXiv URLs. Papers 3-10 are well-known published works.
