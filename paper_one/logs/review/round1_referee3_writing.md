## Referee Report — Presentation Specialist

**Date**: 2026-03-26
**Paper**: "Creative Destruction in the Market for Intelligence: Demand Reallocation When New LLMs Enter"

### 总体评价（Summary Assessment）

This paper is, on the whole, competently written and more readable than the average working paper in empirical IO. The central narrative -- that creative destruction in the LLM API market operates almost exclusively through within-family upgrades rather than cross-firm displacement -- is clearly stated, consistently developed, and genuinely interesting. The authors deserve credit for an Introduction that lays out the question cleanly, a well-motivated three-channel decomposition (cannibalization, displacement, expansion), and commendable transparency about the paper's limitations. The prose is generally direct and economical, the theoretical framework is presented with appropriate parsimony, and the paper reads more like a research article than a technical report -- which is not a given for work in this space.

That said, the paper has two significant presentation weaknesses. First, the Introduction is too long and too front-loaded with results. At roughly 1,800 words across 8 dense paragraphs (lines 55--75), it reads more like an executive summary than an invitation to keep reading. The first three paragraphs are excellent -- they set up the question with concrete facts and clear economic stakes. But paragraphs 4--7 dump the entire empirical strategy, all key coefficients, robustness metrics, and contribution claims before the reader has any context for evaluating them. This is the opposite of the Autor-Dorn-Hanson approach, where the Introduction builds tension and the results section delivers the payoff. Second, while the paper is mostly free of egregious AI-generated prose, there are recurring patterns -- excessive hedging, over-symmetric sentence structures, and a tendency toward exhaustive enumeration where a single sharp sentence would suffice -- that subtly erode readability and signal machine composition.

The tables and figures are functional but underexploited. Several figures appear in the Appendix with one-sentence notes and receive minimal textual discussion. The event study figures, which are the visual heart of the paper's argument, have wide confidence intervals that the text handles honestly but could contextualize more effectively. The Conclusion, while adequate, largely recapitulates the Introduction rather than advancing the argument to a memorable final position. These are fixable problems, and the underlying research is strong enough to merit the effort.

### 逐项评分

1. **叙事结构与故事性：7/10** — The paper tells a clear story: creative destruction in LLM markets runs vertically (within product families), not horizontally (across firms). This is a memorable, citable finding. The three-channel decomposition provides a clean organizing framework, and the paper consistently returns to it. The Schumpeter/Arrow framing is apt and well-deployed. I dock points for two reasons. First, the "story" peaks in the Introduction and then the Results section largely confirms what the reader already knows -- there is no genuine surprise or narrative arc in the body of the paper. A good empirical paper builds toward its punchline; this one delivers the punchline on page 2 and then spends 15 pages verifying it. Second, the connection between the nested logit estimates and the panel regression results is asserted as "mutual validation" (line 483) but never developed into a truly integrated narrative -- they feel like two parallel analyses rather than two acts of a single story.

2. **逻辑连贯性：8/10** — This is the paper's strongest dimension. The logical architecture is sound: theory generates three hypotheses; two empirical approaches (structural demand model, descriptive panel) are deployed; results map back to hypotheses; limitations are acknowledged. The transition from the aggregate null to the family-upgrade channel is the paper's best analytical move -- the decomposition is well-motivated and the punchline is sharp. I deduct points for two gaps. First, the relationship between the nested logit sigma (0.46) and the panel regression family-upgrade coefficient (-0.43) is presented as concordant, but the paper never formally tests whether the structural model's predicted displacement matches the reduced-form estimate -- the claim of "mutual validation" (lines 482--483) rests on an eyeball comparison of ranges. Second, the discussion of the IV failure (lines 393--397) is excellent in diagnosis but creates an unresolved logical tension: if the best available instrument fails, how confident should we be in the OLS estimate that we are asked to treat as the "preferred" result? The Oster bound partially addresses this, but the paper could be more explicit about the direction and likely magnitude of OLS bias.

3. **语言质量：6/10** — The prose is generally clear and direct, well above the modal working paper. Sentence-level clarity is high, jargon is used appropriately, and technical concepts are explained for a broad readership. However, there are recurring patterns that lower the score. (a) Over-hedging: phrases like "is associated with approximately 35% fewer daily requests" (line 43), "produce no detectable displacement" (line 43), "we find suggestive evidence" (line 69) -- the paper hedges so systematically that it occasionally undermines its own claims. Some hedging is appropriate given the descriptive identification, but the paper could be more assertive where the evidence is strong (the family-upgrade result). (b) Exhaustive enumeration: the paper frequently lists three or more parallel constructs where two would suffice (e.g., lines 59: "market structure, innovation incentives, and welfare"; line 61: "upgrade risk... provider risk... hedging strategies"). This ternary rhythm is a hallmark of AI-generated text. (c) Redundancy between sections: the family-upgrade coefficient of -0.43 is reported in the Abstract, Introduction (line 67), Results (line 455), the economic significance paragraph (line 479), the structural validation paragraph (line 483), the Discussion (line 604), and the Conclusion (line 664). Six repetitions of the same number is at least three too many. (d) Some sentences are unnecessarily long; the Abstract is a single 200-word paragraph that tries to report every result.

4. **Introduction 质量：6.5/10** — The first paragraph (line 55) is strong: it opens with a concrete, vivid fact ("nearly 400 models from 66 providers, spanning a 100-fold range in per-token prices") and immediately establishes the empirical setting. The second paragraph (line 57) cleanly defines the three channels. The third paragraph (line 59) connects the question to economic theory (Arrow, Schumpeter) with real intellectual substance. These three paragraphs are close to the Autor-Dorn-Hanson standard. But the Introduction then loses its way. Paragraph 4 (line 61) on developer risk is a digression that belongs in the Discussion -- it breaks the momentum between "why this question matters for economists" and "how we answer it." Paragraphs 5--7 (lines 63--71) are a miniature version of the entire paper: they report the data source, the nested logit estimate with standard errors and an Oster bound, the panel regression coefficients, the family-upgrade decomposition, the alternative nesting results, and the market expansion evidence. By the time the reader reaches the contribution paragraph (line 71), they have already read all the results. This structure eliminates the incentive to read Sections 5--6. A top-tier Introduction should state the question, preview the approach, hint at the answer, and make the reader want to see the evidence -- not lay out every coefficient. The final two paragraphs (lines 73--75) -- the "what we do not do" paragraph and the roadmap -- are competently executed, though the roadmap paragraph is boilerplate that adds nothing.

5. **表格图表：5.5/10** — The tables are clean and professionally formatted (booktabs, threeparttable, table notes). The variable definitions table (Table 1) is a helpful reference. The core regression tables (Tables 3--5) have clear column labels and appropriate notation. However, there are significant weaknesses. (a) Table 2 (nested logit) is the most important table in the paper but receives only two paragraphs of textual discussion for the OLS column and one paragraph for the IV column. The coefficients on context length, reasoning, tool calls, and model age are each discussed in a single sentence (lines 401); a reader who wants to understand why reasoning is negative or why tool calls flip sign between OLS and IV will find no guidance. (b) The event study figures (Figures 11, 16, 12) are the visual centerpiece but have extremely wide confidence bands -- so wide that in Figure 16 (family upgrade, N=8 events with full windows), the confidence interval spans from roughly -5 to +3. The text acknowledges this honestly (line 507) but does not help the reader understand what to take away visually. A figure where the confidence interval is 8 log points wide is not really "showing" anything -- it is showing that you do not have enough data for an event study with that specification. (c) Several Appendix figures (Figures 1, 2, 5, 10) are dumped with one-line notes and never discussed in the text beyond a parenthetical reference. Figure 2 (HHI over time) shows a dramatic artifact -- HHI starts near 10,000 and crashes to under 1,000 in the first two weeks -- that is clearly driven by the panel starting with very few models. This is never explained and could confuse readers. (d) Figure 9 (model entry dynamics) shows a massive spike in new models in week 1 (>300 models) followed by single-digit weekly entries. This is obviously a left-censoring artifact (the panel starts and all pre-existing models are coded as "entering" in week 1), but the figure presents it as if it were a genuine entry surge. This needs a note or redesign. (e) Figure 13 (robustness of sigma) uses bar lengths to represent sigma values, making the near-unity tier-based estimates visually dominate, but the most informative comparison (firm-based sigma with its confidence interval) is visually small. A dot-and-whisker plot would be more effective.

6. **整体可读性：7/10** — A non-specialist economist could read this paper and understand the core finding. The three-channel decomposition is intuitive, the vertical-differentiation interpretation is accessible, and the paper avoids unnecessary formalism. The theory section (Section 3) is appropriately short and clearly presented -- the utility decomposition, share equations, and testable hypotheses are all well-explained. The identification concerns subsection (lines 339--348) is a model of honest, clear-eyed discussion. However, readability suffers from three issues. First, the paper is too long for its content. At roughly 790 lines of LaTeX (including preamble and appendix), the body text runs to perhaps 25 pages. The content -- one nested logit, one panel regression with a decomposition, and robustness checks -- could be presented in 18--20 pages without losing substance. The Related Literature section (lines 97--109) is comprehensive but could be tightened; the five separate \paragraph blocks cover ground that partially overlaps. Second, the Discussion section (Section 7) is long and partly redundant with the Results interpretation that already appears in Section 6. The "economic interpretation" subsection (lines 602--610) repeats the family-upgrade finding for the fifth time. Third, some passages are dense enough to lose a non-specialist reader, particularly the own-price elasticity formula (line 401) and the Oster bounds discussion (line 399), which are important for specialists but break the flow for general readers.

### 总分：6.6/10

### Major Comments（必须回应）

1. **Restructure the Introduction (lines 55--75).** The Introduction currently runs 8 paragraphs and ~1,800 words, front-loading every coefficient and robustness metric. Reduce it to 5--6 paragraphs. Keep paragraphs 1--3 largely intact. Move the developer-risk paragraph (currently para 4, line 61) to the Discussion. Condense paragraphs 5--7 into a single paragraph that previews the approach and hints at the punchline without reporting exact coefficients -- something like: "We find that displacement is concentrated almost entirely in within-family upgrades, with no detectable cross-firm effects." Save the specific numbers for the Results section, where the reader has context to evaluate them.

2. **Reduce redundancy of key results.** The family-upgrade coefficient (-0.43) and nesting parameter (0.46) are each reported 5--6 times across the Abstract, Introduction, Results, Discussion, and Conclusion. Each section should add interpretive value, not merely re-state the number. The Abstract and Conclusion should report the coefficient once. The Results section should develop it in detail. The Discussion should interpret it in economic terms without re-stating it. I suggest a single pass through the paper to mark every instance of "-0.43" and "0.46" and ask whether each repetition adds new information.

3. **Provide substantive discussion of Table 2 coefficients.** Table 2 is the structural demand model -- the paper's first empirical contribution -- but the non-sigma coefficients receive cursory treatment (lines 401). Why is the reasoning dummy negative? The text waves at "higher prices of reasoning models" but this is conditional on the price coefficient, so the negative sign needs a real explanation (unobserved quality? selection into reasoning use cases?). Why does tool-call support flip from insignificant in the logit to significant in the NL-OLS and back to insignificant in the NL-IV? What does a model-age coefficient of -0.006 (NL-OLS) mean economically -- how many daily requests does a model lose per day of aging? These are first-order results that the paper skips over.

4. **Address the Figure 2 and Figure 9 artifacts.** Figure 2 (HHI) shows HHI crashing from ~10,000 to ~600 in the first two weeks, which is an artifact of the panel starting with few models and rapidly adding the pre-existing stock. Figure 9 (model entry) shows >300 "new models" in the first week for the same reason. These figures, presented without explanation, are misleading. Either (a) add prominent notes explaining the left-censoring issue, (b) start the time series after the initial loading period (e.g., from January 2026), or (c) replace them with versions that exclude the startup period. As presented, a reader would reasonably conclude that market concentration fell dramatically and that there was a massive entry wave in late December -- neither of which is the economic phenomenon the paper intends to describe.

5. **Reconsider the event study figure for family upgrades (Figure 16).** With only 8 events having full pre/post windows, the confidence intervals are so wide (spanning roughly 8 log points) that the figure does not visually support the paper's claims. The text (lines 507, 512) handles this honestly, but the figure's visual impression contradicts the narrative. Options: (a) present the 43-event version with shorter pre/post windows; (b) supplement with a simpler before/after comparison (e.g., box plots of predecessor usage in the 7 days before vs. 7--14 days after upgrade); (c) acknowledge more directly that the event study figure is illustrative rather than confirmatory, and that the statistical evidence comes from the panel regression, not the figure.

6. **Strengthen the Conclusion (lines 662--674).** The first two paragraphs of the Conclusion essentially repeat the Introduction and Discussion. A strong Conclusion should do at least one thing the Introduction did not: advance a bold interpretation, identify the single most important open question, or state a clear policy implication. The three "directions for future research" (lines 668--674) are reasonable but generic (longer panels, supply-side data, broader platform coverage). The final paragraph (line 674) -- "much remains to be learned" -- is a weak ending. Consider closing with the paper's most provocative implication: that even with near-zero switching costs, firms in this market are effectively insulated from rivals. What does this tell us about the nature of differentiation in AI? Is this sustainable?

### Minor Comments（建议回应）

1. **Abstract (line 43):** At ~200 words in a single block, the Abstract is dense and hard to scan. Break it into 2--3 shorter paragraphs, or at minimum add a line break between the methodological summary and the results summary. The phrase "A descriptive panel analysis of 385 models over 93 days reveals that creative destruction operates almost exclusively through within-family upgrades" is doing too much work -- split the data description from the finding.

2. **Line 55:** "faced a remarkable menu" -- "remarkable" is an evaluation the reader should make, not the author. Replace with a factual adjective or delete. Suggested: "faced a menu of nearly 400 models..."

3. **Line 59:** "market structure, innovation incentives, and welfare" -- this ternary list appears three more times in the paper in slight variations. Pick the two most important and cut the third, or vary the phrasing.

4. **Line 61:** "Application developers building on LLM APIs face the risk that their chosen model will be displaced or deprecated." This paragraph, while interesting, reads like a consulting report rather than an academic paper. The concepts of "upgrade risk" and "provider risk" are introduced and then never appear again. Either develop them into a proper theoretical contribution or move this paragraph to the Discussion.

5. **Line 73:** "We are explicit about what this paper does not do." This is good practice but the phrasing is somewhat self-congratulatory. Suggested: "Several important caveats apply." or simply begin directly with "Our panel regressions are descriptive, not causal."

6. **Line 75:** The roadmap paragraph ("The remainder of the paper proceeds as follows...") is pure boilerplate. Consider deleting it entirely -- the section headings convey the structure.

7. **Lines 83--87 (Institutional Background):** The sentence "Prices span two orders of magnitude: a frontier reasoning model such as OpenAI's o1 charges roughly $15 per million input tokens, while an efficient open-source model such as DeepSeek-V3 charges $0.14 per million tokens" is vivid and effective. More of this concreteness throughout the paper would improve readability.

8. **Line 115:** "The model provides a parsimonious structure" -- "parsimonious" is doing double duty as both a description and a justification. Simply state: "The nested logit generates testable predictions about substitution patterns and is estimable with the available data."

9. **Lines 156--164 (Hypotheses H1--H3):** Labeling these as "H1," "H2," "H3" with bold formatting is appropriate, but the text surrounding each hypothesis is too long. Each hypothesis should be a single sharp sentence, followed by a brief justification. Currently H1 (lines 158--159) is 4 lines of dense text that mixes the hypothesis with its mechanism.

10. **Line 263:** "A power-law fit to the rank-size distribution of the top 200 models yields an exponent of alpha = 1.57, indicating a concentration level intermediate between classic Zipf's law (alpha = 1) and the more concentrated distributions observed in social media platforms." This sentence is informative but comes out of nowhere -- it is not connected to the preceding or following text. Either integrate it into a broader discussion of market structure or move it to the Appendix.

11. **Line 308:** The outside option discussion is thorough and well-done. The result that sigma is invariant to the outside option share (lines 310) is an important robustness check that could be stated more prominently.

12. **Lines 437:** "The low R-squared does not invalidate the coefficient estimates, but it underscores that entry is a small part of the story of day-to-day usage variation." This is a model sentence -- clear, honest, and informative. More of this tone throughout would improve the paper.

13. **Line 479:** "the within-family upgrade coefficient of -0.43 translates to a 1 - e^{-0.43} approximately 35% decline" -- this back-transformation is helpful and should appear earlier (first mention of the coefficient in the Results section, or even in the Abstract).

14. **Line 586:** "confirming that the null is not driven by one firm's idiosyncratic entry pattern" -- consider showing the leave-one-firm-out results as a coefficient plot rather than relegating them to an Appendix table.

15. **Lines 630--638 (Policy Implications):** These three observations are presented with appropriate caution, but the writing is overly tentative. "We offer three observations, framed with appropriate caution" (line 632) -- the "framed with appropriate caution" clause is unnecessary meta-commentary. Just state the observations.

16. **Table 1 (Variable Definitions):** The variable "SameFamilyUpgrade" is defined as "Indicator for a new model in the same product family (e.g., GPT-4o -> GPT-4o-mini)" -- but GPT-4o-mini is not an upgrade to GPT-4o; it is a cheaper, smaller variant. This example may confuse readers familiar with these models. Use a clearer example (e.g., "Claude 3 Sonnet -> Claude 3.5 Sonnet").

17. **Table 3 (Entry Effects):** The within-R-squared values are reported as 0.000 and -0.000. Negative within-R-squared values are technically possible in fixed-effects regressions but are unusual enough to warrant a footnote explaining that this occurs when the entry variables explain less within-model variation than a model with only fixed effects.

### AI 写作痕迹检查

1. **Ternary enumeration.** The paper systematically groups ideas in threes: "market structure, innovation incentives, and welfare" (line 59); "cannibalization, displacement, expansion" (line 57 -- this one is analytically motivated and fine); "Three additional findings round out the picture" (line 69); "Three specific contributions" (line 71); "three observations" (line 632); "Three directions for future research" (line 668). The ternary pattern is the single most recognizable AI writing signature. Where the grouping is analytically motivated (the three channels), keep it. Where it is presentational (contribution claims, policy implications, future directions), consider reducing to two or varying the structure.

2. **Excessive hedging and qualification.** Nearly every empirical claim is wrapped in distancing language: "is associated with" (used 6 times), "produce no detectable" (lines 43, 67), "no measurable displacement" (line 604), "suggestive evidence" (lines 69, 596), "we find suggestive but imprecise support" (line 626). The paper is descriptive and the authors are right to be cautious -- but the cumulative effect of this hedging is to drain the prose of conviction. In the strongest results (the family-upgrade coefficient), be more direct: "predecessor models lose roughly 35% of daily requests" rather than "the predecessor is associated with approximately 35% fewer daily requests."

3. **Over-balanced sentence structure.** Multiple passages deploy perfectly symmetric constructions: "If cannibalization dominates... If cross-firm displacement dominates... If market expansion dominates..." (lines 59, three parallel sentences of similar length); "upgrade risk... provider risk" (line 61); "$\sigma$ near one implies... $\sigma$ near zero implies..." (line 65). Natural academic writing is not this perfectly balanced. Vary sentence lengths and structures; let some points get more space than others.

4. **Meta-commentary.** The paper frequently narrates its own rhetorical moves: "We are explicit about what this paper does not do" (line 73); "We offer three observations, framed with appropriate caution" (line 632); "This is consistent with H2" (line 475); "These patterns support H2 (quality-ladder concentration)" (line 475); "receives partial support" / "receives strong support" / "receives suggestive but imprecise support" (lines 622--626). This hypothesis-then-verdict structure is characteristic of AI-generated text and is not how economists typically write. Instead of "H2 receives strong support," write: "The data confirm the quality-ladder prediction: displacement is concentrated within model families."

5. **Boilerplate connectors.** "Three additional findings round out the picture" (line 69); "The remainder of the paper proceeds as follows" (line 75); "Two patterns stand out" (line 545); "Several features of the data warrant attention" (line 270); "much remains to be learned" (line 674). These are filler phrases that add no information. Delete them or replace with substantive transitions.

6. **Perfectly parallel limitation lists.** The Limitations subsection (lines 640--656) presents six limitations, each introduced by a bold \paragraph label, each running exactly 3--5 sentences. This uniform packaging suggests automated generation. In natural writing, some limitations merit a full paragraph of discussion (no causal identification) while others can be dispatched in a single sentence (short panel). Weight the discussion by importance.

### 总体建议：MINOR REVISION

The paper addresses a genuinely interesting question with appropriate methods and commendable transparency about its limitations. The core finding -- that creative destruction operates vertically within product families -- is clear, well-supported, and memorable. The presentation problems identified above are real but fixable: a tighter Introduction, reduced redundancy, better figure design, and a pass to remove AI writing artifacts would bring this paper to a publishable standard. The empirical content does not need to change; the packaging does.
